# LastTime

> Remember life upkeep that does not happen every day, without turning it into a to-do list.

LastTime is a calm life maintenance dashboard for the little things that quietly disappear from memory: the last haircut, the last toothbrush replacement, the last time you washed the sheets, cleaned the fridge, scheduled a dental cleaning, or backed up photos.

It is built for people who want a lighter mind, not another productivity system to obey.

[Download LastTime on the App Store](https://apps.apple.com/app/id6778527643)

## The Things You Forget Are Usually Not Daily Things

Most apps are made for tasks, deadlines, habits, and streaks. That works for urgent work. It feels wrong for ordinary life maintenance.

You do not need a red overdue badge because the fridge has not been cleaned recently. You need a quiet place that remembers the fact for you, shows how long it has been, and makes the next action obvious.

LastTime is not a to-do list and not a streak tracker. It focuses on one question:

**When did I last do this?**

That small shift changes the feeling of the whole app. No guilt. No daily pressure. No fake productivity theater. Just the recurring life details you can finally stop keeping in your head.

## Why LastTime Feels Different

### A Dashboard For Real Life

Open the app and see your maintenance items as a clear home dashboard. Each item shows what matters: its name, its last completion, and whether it needs attention now.

Use it for:

- Haircuts
- Toothbrush replacement
- Bed sheet washing
- Fridge cleaning
- Dental cleaning
- Checkups
- Supplement restocks
- Photo backups
- Password updates
- Any custom life upkeep you want to remember

### Calm Attention States

LastTime avoids strict deadline energy. Instead of shouting at you, it gives each item a calm attention state based on its recommended interval.

- Normal, when everything is fine
- Coming up, when it is worth noticing soon
- Needs attention, when it is time to handle it
- Long overdue, when the gap has grown large enough to matter

You get signal without shame. That is the point.

### One Tap To Record Today

When you finish something, tap it once. LastTime records today immediately.

Made a mistake? Undo it. Need to add a past completion instead? Open the item history and record the real date.

The interaction is intentionally small because the moment of maintenance is already over. The app should not make you do more work just to prove you did the work.

### Start Fast With Templates

Blank setup screens are where good intentions go to die. LastTime gives you everyday templates so you can start with useful items in seconds, then customize names, intervals, icons, and colors for your own rhythm.

Templates are only a starting point. Your life maintenance dashboard becomes yours.

### History That Becomes Insight

Every completion becomes part of a private history. Over time, LastTime helps you see patterns that memory alone cannot hold.

- Completion history for each item
- Git-style calendar patterns
- Interval trends between completions
- Rhythm insights over time

This is where the app becomes more than a reminder. It becomes a record of how your real life moves.
